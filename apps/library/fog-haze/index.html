VISIBILITY RESOLVER

Fog, haze and smog remain distinct visual conditions

Fog reduces visibility with cool near-ground layers and softened contrast. Mist is lighter and leaves more sky context visible. Haze uses warmer diffusion, while smog adds pollution tint and aerosol behavior. Freezing fog introduces ice-crystal detail without turning the scene into snowfall.

Smoke, wildfire pollution, dust, sand and volcanic ash receive deeper treatment in the aerosol collection, but the shared visibility resolver ensures that celestial attenuation and safe contrast behave consistently.
