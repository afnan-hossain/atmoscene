LUNAR RESOLVER

Moon phases inside complete weather scenes

Atmoscene treats evening, moonrise, midnight, late night, moonset and pre-dawn as separate inputs from lunar phase. Weather can attenuate the moon and stars without changing the underlying astronomical state or introducing a second celestial body.

New-moon scenes suppress visible surface texture. Illuminated phases retain darker grooved crater detail and inner shading; special states such as pink moon, blood moon and lunar eclipse remain distinct from the standard cycle.

InputNight period + lunar phase

Local time, moon state and event flags establish the astronomical layer.

ComposeMoon + stars + weather

Cloud, fog, snow, smoke and ash attenuate visibility without replacing the phase.

OutputResponsive night scene

Animated SVG with a reduced-motion path and stable text-safe zones.

How do the night animations behave?

Textured lunar phases

Craters use darker surface tones and inset shading, never transparent holes that reveal the sky behind the moon.

Stationary silver stars

Small stars twinkle where they are with only minimal drift; clarity and cloud coverage determine their visibility.

Weather-aware visibility

Cloud cover, fog, snow, smoke and ash reduce lunar glow and star contrast while preserving the correct moon state.
