RESOLVER LOGIC

Sunrise, sunset and daytime weather that read as time

Many weather icon sets reuse one blue sky and one fixed sun. Atmoscene uses solar phase as a first-class scene input: the gradient, light source, glow and solar position progress through the day while cloud, rain, visibility and wind remain independent layers.

The collection combines eight daylight phases with 48 weather and sky states. Applications can request a semantic scene and receive one coherent animated SVG composition, with a static fallback available for reduced motion or export.

InputLocal time + solar phase

Dawn through dusk, with eclipse as a distinct event state.

ComposeSky + sun + weather

One celestial body, protected content zones and phase-aware lighting.

OutputAnimated SVG scene

Lightweight, responsive and ready for cards, dashboards or hero scenes.

What is included?

Eight daylight phases

Dawn, sunrise, morning, noon, afternoon, sunset, dusk and solar eclipse each receive a distinct visual state.

Composable weather

Clear sky, cloud, rain, storm, fog, wind, snow, tropical weather, aerosols and optical phenomena can resolve without changing the phase model.

Celestial safeguards

The resolver prevents a weather overlay from adding a second sun and keeps eclipse artwork separate from ordinary clear-sky scenes.
