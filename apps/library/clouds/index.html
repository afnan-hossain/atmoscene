COVERAGE RESOLVER

Cloud layers that respond to the surrounding atmosphere

Cloud amount affects more than the foreground shape. Atmoscene adjusts cloud depth, scene tint, celestial contrast and motion so a partly cloudy sunrise cannot look identical to an overcast midnight sky.

Cloud layers remain independent from precipitation, visibility and wind. That makes them reusable across clear transitions, rain and storms without embedding a second sun or moon in the condition artwork.

InputCoverage + sky phase

Cloud amount, local daylight or night period and lunar state establish the scene.

ComposeDepth + light + flow

Layer density and contrast respond to the sky while wind controls motion separately.

OutputProtected cloud scene

Responsive SVG keeps celestial artwork proportionate and content-safe zones readable.

Light coverage

Mostly clear skies retain the dominant sun or moon and use restrained cloud motion.

Broken coverage

Partly and mostly cloudy states deepen the layer stack and reduce celestial contrast progressively.

Overcast depth

Dense multi-layer coverage and stronger atmospheric tint communicate a fully covered sky.
